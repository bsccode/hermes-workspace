## Slide 10

SOLE TRADER

It is the simplest form of business structure
It is basically a single purpose operating a business
The sole trader may employ others to work for him/her and operate under a business name
Limitations
No separate existence from the business, hence all liabilities is borne by the person, which means personal assets may be at risk (unlimited liability)


---

## Slide 11

SOLE TRADER

Advantages
Simple to create
Easy to control
Profits are not shared
Management of business
Privacy
Some legal requirements
Australian Business Number (ABN)
GST [income of $75,000 or more]
Business Name Register


---

## Slide 12

PARTNERSHIP

Definition: s7(1) Partnership Act 1895 (WA) partnership is the relation which subsists between persons carrying on a business in common with a view of profit
Persons includes anybody of persons corporate or unincorporated (s3)


---

## Slide 13

PARTNERSHIP

A partnership is essentially a form of contract
Individual partners enter into a partnership agreement on how to conduct the business subject to statutory provisions, mutual rights and obligations governed by the agreement
A partnership agreement may be:
A formally written agreement
Partly written and partly oral; or
May be wholly oral or wholly or partly implied by conduct


---

## Slide 14

PARTNERSHIP

Not a separate legal entity
Unlimited liability except limited partnership
Each partner is liable for their own tax at individual rate based on the share of profit received
Partnerships dissolve automatically on retirement, death or bankruptcy unless the partnership agreement otherwise provides
General partnerships are limited to 20 partners


---

## Slide 15

PARTNERSHIP

A partnership does not exist unless each of the elements in the statutory definition is satisfied: s 7(1) (WA):
An existing relationship
Between persons
Carrying on business in common
With view of profit


---

## Slide 16

TRUST

A trust exists when one party is required to hold or invest property on behalf of another
Not a separate legal entity
It is governed by Trustees Act 1962 (WA)


---

## Slide 17

TRUST

The trustee is personally responsible for the debts and liabilities of the trust
For tax purposes it is not recognised as a separate legal entity
Tax is either payable in the hands of the beneficiaries or if income is not distributed, the trustee is personally liable for any tax at penalty rates

## Slide 18

INCORPORATED AND UNINCORPORATED ASSOCIATIONS

A not-for-profit association is a group of two or more people who have agreed to join together to pursue a common lawful purpose of some kind


---

## Slide 19

NOT-FOR-PROFIT

Not-for-profit includes:
Social and sporting clubs
Artistic and learned societies
Trade and professional associations
Educations, religious or charitable organisations; and
Associations formed to promote cultural, environmental or other purposes intended to benefit the community
A not-for-profit organisation can be incorporated or unincorporated


---

## Slide 20

UNINCORPORATED NOT-FOR-PROFIT ASSOCIATION

Simplest form of unincorporated organisation is a not-for-profit association such as a social or sporting club which is formed on the basis that any profits made are used for the purposes of which the association is formed and distribution of profits is forbidden
Limitations
Cannot buy or own property
Enter into a contract or be liable in tort
Sue or be sued
Receive a gift, be the beneficiary of a trust or be left property by will
It can only act through its members


---

## Slide 21

INCORPORATED ASSOCIATIONS

Benefits are obtained through registration under the associations incorporation legislation which is under State based legislations [Associations Incorporation Act 2015 WA]
It is designed for not-for-profit associations
Members are protected from personal liability
It can own assets and enter into contracts
Except in limited circumstances, it cannot be incorporated to make profits solely
It must have a constitution either adopting the model or own


---

## Slide 22

COMPANIES

Companies are classified in accordance to the liability of members
There are two classifications in essence:
Proprietary
Public
Proprietary:
Limited by shares
Unlimited with a share capital
Public:
Limited by shares
Unlimited with a share capital
Limited by guarantee
No liability


---

## Slide 23

LIMITED BY SHARES

The most common form of companies
Here members (shareholders) liability is limited to the amount invested in the company

NO LIABILITY

## Slide 26

LIMITED BY GUARANTEE

Not required to contribute any capital unless and until there is a shortfall when the company is wound-up
The constitution will need to prescribe the amount of the guarantee – normally it will be a nominal sum e.g. $10
