WHAT IS INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY (IP)?

Intellectual property” is a generic term for the various rights or bundles of rights which the law accords for the protection of creative effort – or, more especially, for the protection of economic investment in creative effort.”
“Intellectual property” refers to the various intangible subject matter protected under a loose collection of legal titles.”
It is a concept that is familiar yet elusive to a definition.
Generally, it is a broad and generic term used to refer to bundles of rights granted by the law to safeguard creative efforts.

## Slide 16

COPYRIGHT PROTECTED EXPRESSIONS - ELEMENTS

Copyright automatically exists in works when all of the criteria for subsistence are satisfied. In essence:
Reduce ideas to material form
That material form is a recognised subject matter for protection
The expression is original/authored 
Some connecting factor between the work and Australia
Not all of these criteria apply to Part IV subject matter

## Slide 18

COPYRIGHT ELEMENT 1 – MATERIAL FORM

S 32(1) – copyright subsists in a work that is ‘made’ (or published)
Works are ‘made’ when reduced to writing or some other ‘material form’: s22(1)
S22(2): works in the form of recorded sounds are in material form
Note - copyright subsistence is NOT dependent on a work being published
The continuum is:
Idea --- material form --- (perhaps) publication

## Slide 38

PT IV SUBJECT MATTER - SOUND RECORDINGS

Sound recording is defined in s.10(1) of the Act to mean “the aggregate of the sounds embodied in a record”.  
Record means “a disc, tape, paper or other device in which sounds are embodied”.
Section 24 provides that sounds are regarded as ‘embodied’ in a record if the sounds are capable of being reproduced from the record with or without the aid of some other device.


---

## Slide 39

PT IV SUBJECT MATTER – CINEMATOGRAPH FILMS

Defined in s.10(1) to mean “the aggregate of the visual images embodied in an article or thing so as to be capable by the use of that article or thing -
(a) of being shown as a moving picture; or
(b) of being embodied in another article or thing by the use of which it can be so shown,
and includes the aggregate of the sounds embodied in a sound-track associated with such visual images”.
‘sound track’ in relation to a film is also defined in s 10(1)

## Slide 40

PT IV SUBJECT MATTER – BROADCASTS

“‘broadcast’ means a communication to the public delivered by a broadcasting service within the meaning of the Broadcasting Services Act 1992”: s 10(1)
“‘television broadcast’ means visual images broadcast by way of television, together with any sounds broadcast for reception along with those images”
“‘sound broadcast’ means sounds broadcast otherwise than as part of a television broadcast”
‘broadcast’ refers to both TV and sound broadcasts: s25(1)

QUESTION 1

Samantha Lee has recently opened a smoothie shop in Sydney named “Smoothie Haven,” specializing in “Delicious and Nutritious Smoothies.”
To create her menu, Samantha used ChatGPT to "generate a menu for a smoothie shop, including the ingredients for each smoothie." She then enhanced the AI-generated menu, transforming simple ingredient lists into engaging descriptions like “Our Tropical Bliss Smoothie will refresh you with its exotic flavors.”
Instructions to the AI:
"Generate a detailed and unique menu for a smoothie shop named 'Smoothie Haven,' providing a list of ingredients for each smoothie. Include engaging and descriptive language for each menu item to highlight the deliciousness and nutritional benefits of the smoothies. Additionally, create new and unique names for each smoothie based on the revised recipes.“

## Slide 43

QUESTION 1

Three months later, Samantha decided to improve the smoothie recipes by trying all the menu items again, mixing and matching fruits, and changing 90% of the ingredients in each of the AI-generated recipes. She then asked ChatGPT to regenerate the names of the smoothies.
Instructions to the AI:
"Generate a unique name for the attached list of ingredients for each smoothie."
Samantha wants to ensure that her menu remains unique and cannot be easily copied by her competitors. Please advise Samantha.


---

## Slide 44

QUESTION 1 ANSWER

The issue in this question whether Copyright subsists in Samantha’s AI generated recipe and the name of the smoothie. Notably, every section mentioned below refers to the CR Act, unless mentioned otherwise
For CR to subsists, 4 elements have to be satisfied. First, the expression has to be reduced into material form, such expression has to be falls within 4 recognised works listed in s 32 and 4 Pt IV subject matter, the expression has to be original and such work has to be connected to Australia. Materia form includes any form of storage of expression which has to be “permanent” to a certain extent to be reproduced: s 10(1); Chapman Kelly case. According to s 32, works include literary works, dramatic, musical and artistic. Literary work is further defined in s 10(1) to include ‘a table, expressed in words, figures or symbols.’ For copyright to subsists in a work, an element of originality has to be present: s 32(1). Works are deemed to be original if it is not uncopied and an independent intellectual effort is being inputted in generating the work: s 32(1) and Desktop Marketing case. According to the case of Telstra Corporation Ltd and Achos Pty Ltd, the court has held that, in relation to computer generated works, the more an author put the responsibility to the software tool to generate such work, the less likely it could be considered as original. In other words, the more independent intellectual effort was being inputted in to the software, the more likely the work could satisfy the element of originality. Lastly, the work has to be produced by a qualified person in Australia: s 32.


---

## Slide 45

QUESTION 1 ANSWER

Applying the above legal principles into the question, Samantha has inputted the prompt into chatgpt in Australia and the outcome generally fulfils the elements of material form and connecting factor, assuming Samantha has not deleted the chat: s 10(1), s 32. The generated outcome further fulfils the s 10(1) definition of literary work as the recipe generated is being expressed in words. The main issue lies with whether Samantha’s AI generated satisfied the requirement of independent intellectual effort: Telstra Corporation Ltd case and Achos Pty Ltd case. For the first prompt, it appears that Samantha did not input substantial effort in writing the instruction, given the general nature of the inputted prompt. However, distinguishing the first prompt with the second prompt, Samantha’s effort in reviewing and changing 90% of the recipe could satisfy the “originality” element: Desktop Marketing case. As for generating the name of the smoothie, it appears that the effort in generating such names lies heavily on ChatGPT, instead of Samantha and hence failing the “originality” elements. 
In conclusion, given the “originality” element fails in the first prompt in generating the recipe and both of the prompts in generating the name of the smoothie, CR could not subsist. However, CR could subsists in the recipe which Samantha created 3 months later.
