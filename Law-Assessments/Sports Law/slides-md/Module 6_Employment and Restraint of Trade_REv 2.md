## Slide 16

TERMS – EXPRESS VS IMPLIED

The contract of employment sets out the terms of the employment agreement (Express terms). There are also implied terms of a contract:
Duties of the employer
The duty to pay the agreed remuneration.
A duty to provide work.
A duty to take reasonable care to ensure the safety of employees.
Duties of an employee
A duty to obey the employer’s lawful and reasonable instructions.
A duty to work in a skilful and competent manner.
A duty to hand over inventions made during the course of employment.
A duty to disclose information that is relevant to the employer.
A duty to maintain confidentiality.

## Slide 18

UNIVERSITY OF WESTERN AUSTRALIA V GRAY

In 1985 the University of Western Australia (UWA) appointed Dr Bruce Gray as its Professor of Surgery. He was a fulltime employee of UWA and remained as such until March 1997 when he changed to a fractional 30% appointment focussing on clinical work at the Royal Perth Hospital (RPH). 
In his time as a fulltime employee, Dr Gray was required by his terms of appointment to teach and to conduct and stimulate research. The terms incorporated the Statutes and regulations of UWA including its Patents  Regulations.

## Slide 37

EMPLOYEE VS INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR - TEST

In all the circumstances, is the individual working on their own account or for an employer?
Control test: Stevens v Brodribb Sawmilling Co. Pty Ltd; ALHMWU (WA) v Commissioner of Main Roads
Is the person able to exercise control and discretion as to how they perform their responsibilities?  Where the employer has a right to direct what the person does and how the person shall do a task, that person is generally an employee. 
What factors indicate control?  Tasks allocated to them by the employer? Time to perform the task? Due date for the task? Instructions for the task? 
Multi Facet Test
Consider the “totality of the relationship”  Provision of equipment? Hours or work? Provision of holiday and leave? Provision of uniform?  How are the salary being paid – via invoice or fortnight? 
Hollis v Vabu Pty Ltd (2001) 207 CLR 21
