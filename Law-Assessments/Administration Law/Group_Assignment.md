**Bibliography/footnotes:** No, but cases referred to must be cited in-text (like they do in court decisions/judgments)

**Instructions**

This is an exercise in writing up and presenting an outline of submissions in the Federal Court which almost every lawyer does at some point in their career (if not in the Federal Court than some other Court such as the WA Supreme Court or District Court, either for an interlocutory hearing, a trial or an appeal). Those students who have done mooting will be familiar with drawing up submissions.

You will need to refer to the *Federal Court Rules 2011* (FCR) and *Federal Court Practice Notes* for the content of your outline of submissions and the formatting of your document in terms of font size and margins, etc. For comparison, you might want to look at the Supreme Court of WA Consolidated Practice Directions PD 2.1 on Submissions which you will probably come across a fair bit in your career practising in this jurisdiction and which you will gain some familiarity with in the Civil Procedure and Practice unit, but for this exercise, you should be guided by the Federal Court Rules as this case, involving judicial review under the *Administrative Decisions Judicial Review Act 1977* (Cth) ('*ADJR* Act') of an administrative decision purportedly made under legislation, is definitely something that is in the Federal Court's wheelhouse.

You will find numerous completed examples on the net as to what outlines of submissions look like in the Federal Court as well as the formal setting out of such documents with the parties and action number appearing on the front page. There is a degree of flexibility in how submissions are drawn up as you will probably gather from the examples you come across that will not be identical in how they are set out and worded, but the structure and content outlined in the FCR is the main thing to adhere to.

You will note that the page limit of 10 pages is actually dictated by the rules themselves. If, per chance, your submissions do exceed this amount, you should draft a letter to the Federal Court registry for lodging with your submission document seeking the court's leave to accept for filing your document notwithstanding the fact that it exceeds the prescribed 10 page limit.

**(Practice Tip: Whenever you want to do something not strictly in accordance with the court rules, you can and should seek the court's leave -- a useful thing to know as court's have a wide discretion to bend their own rules where it is in the 'interests of justice' to do so -- but it is just that, a discretion, not a right).**

Students who are doing Constitutional Law this semester will recognise the scenario below which is also used in that unit, but from the perspective of mounting a constitutional challenge to the decision, not a challenge based on judicial review which is what this exercise is concerned with. Some details from that scenario such as the legislation passed by the fawning WA Parliament and the International Convention against Wokeness have been omitted from the facts below as they are not relevant to the judicial review challenge. Therefore, tempting as it will be to raise constitutional challenges in your submission for this assignment, you will confine your arguments to judicial review and the principles taught in this unit. There would be two separate federal court applications, one a Form 66 for judicial review which is what this exercise is about, and another application filed at the same time challenging the constitutionality of the impugned legislation. These arguments would no doubt be heard on different days by the court -- the constitutional law challenge first because if that succeeds, that is the end of the matter and there is no need to consider the judicial review aspect (as the Act under which the decision has been made is invalid, thus no valid decision. Sweet!)

**Factual Scenario**

The year is 2030 and One Nation won the federal election in 2028 and formed a majority government with its leader Pauline Hanson appointed as Prime Minister. (As I write this in January 2026, a national poll puts them at 22%, ahead of the beleaguered Coalition on 21%, and not too far behind Labour on 32%, so it's not out of the question!)

Inspired by events in the US, One Nation had campaigned on a platform of 'Killing off DEI' in their war on wokeism. The electorate seem to have responded positively to this campaign and voted in One Nation by a landslide.

One bill that One Nation (hereinafter referred to as 'the Federal Government') introduced into Parliament which passed without amendment by a bare majority was the *Make Academic Freedom Great Again By Killing It Bill 2030 (the Kill Bill).* In this Bill, tertiary education institutions are not permitted to allow the formation of groups within their institutions deemed to be 'problematic groups'.

Section 2 of the *Kill Bill* defines 'problematic groups' to be:

> Any group that is declared by the responsible Minister of Education ('the Minister') to be a 'problematic group' which is a group that in the reasonable opinion of the Minister has been formed to advance the causes of minorities including but not limited to ethnic groups, religious groups LGBTQI+, neurodiverse, disabled or feminist groups, or any group of whatever composition that engages in any form of political protest or political discussion on campus, whether or not there is a likelihood of harm to persons or property arising out of such activities.

The *Kill Bill's* objects are stated in Section 3 to be :

> To prevent disruption to the education of the general population of students in higher education institutions by problematic groups, and focusing all students on their studies which is the core function of higher education institutions.

However, the federal government has no problems with tertiary education institutions permitting the formation and registration with higher education institutions of clubs to pursue purely recreational activities as this is seen as not disruptive to education.

When the *Kill Bill* passed into law, it was cited as the *Kill Bill Act 2030*. (Technically no longer a Bill now but the name stuck, so the *Kill Bill Act 2030* it is!)

Your firm has been approached by a first year law student, Eddie Munson, enrolled at a private university which is a body incorporated by a WA statute, similar to the University of Notre Dame, Fremantle, but it is a secular university, not a faith based university like Notre Dame (Catholic).

Eddie and a fellow group of 'Dungeons and Dragons' (D&D) gaming enthusiasts form and register a club at your university to meet and play D&D. However, when they meet to play, they often discuss politics and game characters are named after your Australian political heroes of all stripes (eg Wizard Whitlam, Howard the Hammer, Rampaging Rudd, Gladiator Gillard, Morrison the Marauder, Dutton the Destroyer, etc). You are after all, as the saying goes, a 'broad church' in regards to your political views.

The group receives an email from the Vice Chancellor (VC) saying that it has come to their attention that the group has formed a political group, as evidenced by the group's discussions and activities during their gaming sessions and are thus required to cease and desist forthwith, failing which the University will deregister the group as it has been reported to the relevant State and Federal Ministers and declared by them to be a 'Problematic Group' under the relevant federal, and continuation of the group's activities could be met with severe sanctions such as fines and suspension from the university. The VC is aware of the group's activities as gaming sessions are recorded on Panopto which are uploaded to the general university server and made publicly accessible to ensure members of the group who are not able to attend sessions can see the hijinks the group gets up to in their absence and keep up with the game's progress.

You can assume that the Minister made their declaration based on the information passed on to them by the VC about the club's activities, including the Panopto recordings, and that the Minister has at no time informed D&D Inc of its intention to make the declaration and indeed has only informed the VC and not D&D Inc itself about the Declaration.

**Your Task -- Written Component (15%)**

You bring an action in the Federal Court on behalf of your client, D&D Inc (the Applicant) seeking an injunction against the Commonwealth Education Department as Respondent. You have four lines of attack, all of which you should address:

- The decision by the Minister to declare the Applicant a 'problematic group' under s2 of the *Kill Bill Act 2030* (Cth) ('the Decision') is one that is one to which the *ADJR Act* applies having regard to the threshold issues of jurisdiction and standing (you do not need to address the other threshold issues such as justiciability and amenability of the decision maker to judicial review as these are a given, although you should identify what the decision is and what statutory provision it is purportedly made under as background to your submission).

- That the Decision is ultra vires s2 of the *Kill Bill Act 2030* (Cth) in the narrow sense, substantively and/or procedurally and outside the scope of that Act;

- That the Decision is ultra vires s2 of the *Kill Bill Act 2030* (Cth) in the broad sense, on one or more of the various grounds that amount to an abuse of discretionary power and/or failure to exercise discretionary power;

- That the decision is ultra vires in that it infringes principles of procedural fairness in terms of one or more of the following grounds: breach of the hearing rule, rule against bias, no evidence, or a failure to provide adequate reasons for decision.

Assume the matter has been set down for trial. It is now time to do an outline of submissions setting out the basis of the court's jurisdiction to hear the matter, the background of the case, the issues being addressed as outlined above, and your legal arguments to address these issues (and, although not strictly required, any other smarty pants arguments you think you can come up with!), as well as seek appropriate remedies -- in other words, the things the FCR say you should include in your submission.

You will need to make up some facts, in particular the name of the university you attend (we don't want to drag ECU into this as they are a government run university, not a private university, and they surely wouldn't do anything like this!) as well as relevant dates.

The following are some tips I gave to last year's class when presenting your submissions which are worth bearing in mind.

1\. Always introduce yourself and co-counsel and advise for whom you are appearing (ie the Applicant). Most counsel like to begin with 'If it please the Court'. Sounds nice.

2\. Refer to yourself and your co-counsel by their surnames or full names, not just by their Christian names (so the record shows who counsel appearing are in those proceedings)

3\. Refer to the party you are representing as 'the Applicant' or the 'Respondent' rather than 'my/our client'

4\. Instead of saying 'we seek' or 'it is our position' say 'the Applicant/Respondent seeks' or  'it is the Applicant's/Respondent's position' etc

5\. Do not refer to co-counsel as 'my learned friend' but rather as 'my colleague'. The term 'my learned friend' or simply 'my friend' is usually reserved for opposing counsel. If the other party is unrepresented,  just refer to them as 'the Applicant/Respondent' or 'Mr....' Or 'Ms......'

6\. Dress appropriately (males tie and jacket, females business attire)

7\. Do not wear sunglasses in court (although it can be a bit tricky with glasses that automatically darken -may need to tell the associate about that beforehand so the judge knows you're not some gangster or something)

8\. Do not wear glasses/sunglasses on the top of your head

9\. Do not chew gum

10\. Try to maintain eye contact - look up every now and then if reading from notes and look at camera rather than screen if online

11\. Try to speak clearly and at an appropriate pace (and do not speed up the recording)

I think that was about it. Oh, and sign off with something like: 'If it please the court, unless there is anything further I can assist you with, those are the Applicant's submissions' or some other fawning nonsense of that kind!
