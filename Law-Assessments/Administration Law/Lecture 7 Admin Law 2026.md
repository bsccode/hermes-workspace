This ground focuses on the processes followed regarding a decision made rather than the subject matter it addresses. Thus, failure to follow a particular prescribed step in making a decision could result in that decision being void and ultra vires in the narrow/simple sense as if such a decision was never made. This usually relates to an administrative decision but could relate to a legislative decision if a piece of delegated legislation must follow a –particular process in the making of it such as gazetting or tabling in parliament (although we saw in the lecture on delegated legislation, failure to consult and other steps in the making of DL is saved by statute such as the Legislation Act 2003 rather than being considered fatal).
