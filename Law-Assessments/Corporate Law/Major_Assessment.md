**Arka Energy Ltd v Meridian Capital Ltd**

Arka Energy Ltd ("Arka") is a public company incorporated in **India** in 2015. The company specialises in advanced lithium battery storage systems used in large-scale solar energy projects. Arka has four directors:

- Rohan Mehta -- Managing Director

- Kavita Rao -- Technical Director

- Suresh Iyer -- Non-Executive Director

- Ananya Singh -- Independent Director

The company secretary is Neha Kapoor.\
Rohan Mehta is widely recognised as the public face of Arka. He regularly represents the company at international energy forums and is typically the primary contact for investors and commercial partners.\
Internally, Arka's board had resolved that Rohan's authority to enter into contracts was limited to INR 25 million (approximately AUD 500,000). Any agreement exceeding this amount required prior board approval and execution by two directors or one director and the company secretary. This restriction is contained in the company's constitution but was not publicly disclosed.

**The Transaction**

Meridian Capital Ltd ("Meridian") is a public company incorporated in **Western Australia**, specialising in infrastructure and renewable energy investments. Meridian has three directors:

- James Whitaker -- Executive Chairman

- Andrew Collins -- Managing Director

- Rebecca Hughes -- Non-Executive Director

In early 2024, Meridian approached Arka after Rohan presented at a renewable energy conference in Perth. During his presentation, Rohan stated that he "leads all strategic partnerships and commercial negotiations for Arka's international expansion."\
Following the conference, Meridian's Managing Director, Andrew Collins, commenced negotiations directly with Rohan regarding a proposed investment to develop solar farm projects in Western Australia, using Arka's battery storage systems.\
Several meetings were held in Perth, followed by email exchanges. Rohan emphasised that Arka operated with a "lean approval structure" that allowed it to "move quickly on strategic opportunities."\
During negotiations, Meridian's legal advisor asked whether board approval would be required for the proposed deal. Rohan replied via email:

"All internal approvals are standard and will be taken care of. You can proceed on the basis that I have full authority."

After several rounds of negotiation, Rohan sent Meridian a **Heads of Agreement** granting Meridian exclusive rights to deploy Arka's battery systems across specified solar farm sites in Western Australia for a period of 10 years.\
The Heads of Agreement expressly provided that:

"This agreement is governed by the law of Western Australia."

The document was executed using **DocuSign**, with only Rohan's digital signature appearing on behalf of Arka. No second director or company secretary signed the document.\
The agreed upfront investment was **AUD 1.5 million**.\
Meridian transferred the funds to an account specified by Rohan labelled:

"Arka Energy -- Projects Account"

This account differed slightly from Arka's usual corporate account name. Meridian's finance team noted the difference but accepted Rohan's explanation that it was a "designated account for international project transactions."

**Events After Execution**

Two weeks later, Meridian issued a public announcement regarding its partnership with Arka.\
Kavita Rao and the other directors of Arka first became aware of the arrangement through this announcement. Kavita immediately contacted Neha Kapoor, who confirmed that she had seen a draft of the Heads of Agreement but had not been informed that it had been finalised or executed.\
An urgent board meeting was convened. The board resolved that:

- Rohan had exceeded his authority

- No board approval had been granted

- The execution did not comply with the company's constitutional requirements

Arka's lawyers subsequently wrote to Meridian stating that:

- the Heads of Agreement was unauthorised and unenforceable

- the company was prepared to refund the AUD 1.5 million, subject to deductions for administrative costs

Meridian rejected this position.
